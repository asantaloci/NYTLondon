Diversity in London's DNA

London's diversity demographic analysis of who, where, and why.

A map made up of the passports of the most most common non-British nationalities in every London borough. Yanko Tihov

By Angel Santaloci

Oct. 22, 2019

London is known for many things - its tasty food, famous attractions, established history - yet what makes London particularly different by comparison to any other major city is its plethora of cultural diversity.

Recent data shows that nearly a third of London's population is made up of immigrants from all over the world. Below is the world map displaying the population samples of where Londoner's countries of birth originated, not including London.

A map of the concentrations of Londoners based on their country of birth.

The 8 largest populations of the immigrants coming into London are India, Bangladesh, Poland, Romania, Italy, Pakistan, Ireland, and Nigeria. However, this percentage only scratches the tip of the iceberg, being only % of the total London population. Within these largest populations, the standout in this specific data shown below is India, which brings in more than double the amount of people than the second most largest country going to London, Bangladesh (262,247 compared to 109,948 people).

The top 8 largest populations of out of country citizens in London.

The distribution of minority groups is an aspect to take into consideration when defining areas with cultural identity. The graph below displays the different boroughs of London and establishes which communities are predominantly identified as asian, black, or mixed/other when comparing minority demographics of certain areas. The areas which are purple signify even distribution of these minority groups within the borough, while the more pure key colors indicate high saturation of certain groups in a specific borough.

As seen in the data provided, there is a more even distribution between the different ethnicity groups towards the center (City of London), yet there are small areas of boroughs that seem more concentrated with a certain ethnic group compared to others, such as the west side of London having a larger asian population compared to black and others. This correlation suggests the creation of specific ethnic communities within these certain boroughs.

Incentives to move internationally range from London being a global city and financial hub to fostering a better qualities of life for immigrants. No matter the reason, London has a diverse community that is appealing to travelers, as well as current and future Londoners to find a footing in the big city.
